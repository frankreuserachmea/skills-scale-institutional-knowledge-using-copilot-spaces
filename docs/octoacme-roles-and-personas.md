# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape the experience and usability of products. They bridge customer needs and technical implementations to ensure intuitive workflows and high satisfaction.

### Responsibilities
- Design user experience flows and interfaces
- Conduct usability studies and integrate feedback into product iterations
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to specify requirements and validate technical feasibility
- Create design artifacts (wireframes, prototypes, UI specs)

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and consistent user experiences
- Accelerate iteration based on customer feedback
- Reduce usability-related rework

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with team and stakeholders
- Regular syncs with Product and Development
- Annotated mockups, prototypes, and usability findings

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts provide the team with insights for prioritization, impact measurement, and continuous improvement by analyzing qualitative and quantitative data.

### Responsibilities
- Gather, clean, and analyze data related to product usage and outcomes
- Build dashboards to track key metrics
- Support decision-making for Product and Project Managers
- Present findings and recommendations to stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver actionable insights to maximize product impact
- Enable data-driven prioritization and measurable improvements

### Typical Communication
- Dashboard updates
- Ad hoc analyses and reports for initiatives
- Participation in planning and review meetings

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, protect the team from distractions, and ensure continuous process improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Lead daily standups and sprint ceremonies (planning, demos, retrospectives)
- Identify and resolve impediments with the team
- Coach team in agile principles and practices
- Foster a culture of improvement and safety

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and morale
- Identify bottlenecks and drive improvements
- Ensure agile discipline and value delivery

### Typical Communication
- Ceremony facilitation
- Process improvement feedback with PMs and leads
- Coaching/team syncs

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers orchestrate release planning and coordinate end-to-end delivery, ensuring high-quality deployments through structured processes and communication.

### Responsibilities
- Plan, coordinate, and communicate release activities across teams
- Liaise with QA, DevOps, and stakeholders to ensure pre-release requirements are met
- Monitor and document release progress, issues, and outcomes
- Manage rollback and incident response protocols

### Goals
- Ensure smooth, visible, and low-risk releases
- Reduce deployment failures and last-minute surprises
- Maintain a clear release calendar and communication

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings
- Release notes and status broadcasts
- Escalation of blockers and incident coordination

---

## Customer Support Representative

### Role Summary
Customer Support Representatives bring the voice of the user to the team, providing frontline insight into recurring problems and opportunities.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and summarize customer feedback, issues, and feature requests
- Assist users and respond to inbound support queries
- Relay critical customer-impacting issues to Product and Project Management for triage
- Document common problems and solutions

### Goals
- Shorten time to problem resolution for users
- Provide actionable feedback to drive feature and process improvements
- Foster a positive customer relationship

### Typical Communication
- Issue/ticket escalations
- Feedback synthesis in planning meetings
- Collaboration with Product, Project, and QA for root-cause analysis

## Interaction Guidance

All roles are expected to interact regularly to clarify priorities, address blockers, share feedback, and ensure alignment. For larger and complex projects, the following additional practices are recommended:

- **Role assignment checklist** (see below) to ensure accountability is clear at project start
- Regular review of contact points and communication responsibilities
- Collaboration artifacts (e.g., mockups, dashboards, risk logs) should be versioned and accessible

---

### Project Role Assignment Checklist

- [ ] All required roles assigned and documented in project onboarding
- [ ] Stakeholder and support escalation contacts are listed
- [ ] Purpose and communication cadence for each role agreed upon
- [ ] RACI or similar accountability matrix created and shared with team
